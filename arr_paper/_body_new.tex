\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Multi-agent reinforcement learning in environments where agents differ in preferences or roles arises in cooperative AI, mechanism design, and economic simulation \citep{zhang2021multi,leibo2017multi,perolat2017multi}. The Markov-game model \citep{shapley1953stochastic,littman1994markov} captures sequential interaction but treats agents as exchangeable in type. In practice agents have private latent attributes -- foremost, their reward functions -- and reasoning under this uncertainty is the defining feature of Bayesian games \citep{harsanyi1968games}, yet remains under-developed in the sequential MG setting with explicit regret guarantees.

Posterior sampling for RL (PSRL) \citep{strens2000bayesian,osband2013more,agrawal2017optimistic,osband2017posterior} has produced near-optimal regret in single-agent MDPs. Extensions to two-player \emph{zero-sum} MGs were given by \citet{jafarnia2021learning,xiong2022self,yueh2026bayesian}, and cooperative parallel-MDP exploration by \citet{hsu2024randomized}. None addresses the natural cooperative-AI regime where agents are heterogeneous in reward type \emph{and} the learner must infer both transitions and the joint type profile.

\textbf{Contributions.} (1) We formalise the HT-MG-BC framework and prove a \emph{posterior decoupling theorem} (Theorem~\ref{thm:decoupling}): under type-independent transitions and own-type reward locality, the joint posterior over $(P, \boldsymbol{\theta})$ exactly factorises across the transition kernel and the per-agent type marginals at every episode. The factorisation gives storage savings exponential in $n$ and enables the algorithmic primitive in (3). (2) We propose H-PSMG (Algorithm~\ref{alg:hpsmg}) and prove a Bayesian-regret bound $\tilde O(H^{3/2}\sqrt{S A_{\mathrm{joint}} K} + H \sqrt{|\Theta_i| K})$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:main-regret-informal}); without prior factorisation, any algorithm has $\Omega(\sqrt{|\Theta|^n K})$ regret (lower bound, Appendix~\ref{app:lower-no-pf}), so the improvement is exponential in $n$. A matching minimax lower bound (Appendix~\ref{app:lower-matching}) certifies near-optimality. (3) We propose H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} (Algorithm~\ref{alg:hpsmg-plus}), which augments H-PSMG with a \emph{type-discrimination bonus} computable only from the factored marginals. On a deep-trap HT-MG family the bonus cuts burn-in regret from $\Omega(|\Theta_i|^n H)$ to $O(H)$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:hpsmg-plus}). (4) We validate on three LLM substrates: HP-SPGG (controlled, by us), Concordia (independent, DeepMind), and SOTOPIA-Hard (adversarial to our assumptions). The H-PSMG family beats the best published LLM-coordination baselines (ECON, A-ToM) by $6$--$12\times$ across four backbones, the $\beta$-sweep reproduces all three regimes the theorem predicts (matched to per-backbone posterior concentration speed), wins or ties oracle on $16/16$ Concordia configs, and dominates evaluator scores on SOTOPIA-Hard. Full formal proofs, the BPD worked example, counter-examples, $n$-player extension and a function-approximation generalisation appear in the appendix.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Posterior sampling.} PSRL \citep{strens2000bayesian,osband2013more,agrawal2017optimistic,ouyang2017learning} attains near-optimal Bayesian regret in single-agent MDPs, with the information-theoretic framework of \citet{russo2014learning,russo2016information} underpinning our type-inference analysis. In Markov games, posterior sampling has so far been restricted to zero-sum: \citet{jafarnia2021learning} for average-reward, \citet{xiong2022self} with function approximation, and \citet{yueh2026bayesian} for episodic finite-horizon. All assume known type structure.

\textbf{Latent-type and heterogeneous MARL.} \citet{kwon2021rl} prove that learning latent MDPs is exponentially hard without identifiability; \citet{tian2024learning} extend hardness to adaptive adversaries. \citet{albrecht2013game,albrecht2019convergence} introduce Harsanyi-Bellman Ad-hoc Coordination, a Bayesian opponent-type method with asymptotic convergence but no finite-time regret. Our decoupling theorem and $\tilde O(\sqrt K)$ bound provide the finite-sample complement.

\textbf{General-sum equilibrium learning and PSRO.} A line on V-learning and its variants establishes sample complexity for CCE in general-sum MGs \citep{jin2021v,song2022when,mao2023decentralized,erez2023regret,cui2025incentivize}; the PSRO family \citep{lanctot2017unified,muller2019generalized,mcaleer2020pipeline,marris2021multi,mcaleer2021xdo,mcaleer2022anytime,dinh2022online,zhou2022efficient,yao2023policy} targets equilibrium of an empirical game via population learning. Neither line maintains a Bayesian posterior over latent payoff structure; our framework is complementary and PSRO-style oracles can be plugged into the inner CCE step of H-PSMG without affecting the decoupling result.

\textbf{Structured Bayesian RL.} \citet{mutti2024exploiting} factor dynamics via causal priors; \citet{arumugam2026toward} revive PSRL for LLM agents. Neither factors the prior across agent types.

\section{Problem Setting}
\label{sec:setting}

\paragraph{Heterogeneous-Type Markov Game.}
An HT-MG is a tuple
$\mathcal{G} = \langle \mathcal{S}, \{\mathcal{A}_i\}, P^*, \{\Theta_i\}, \{r_i\}, P_0, H \rangle$
with finite state space $|\mathcal S|=S$, joint action space $\mathcal A=\prod_i \mathcal A_i$ of size $A_{\mathrm{joint}}$, type space $\Theta=\prod_i \Theta_i$, transition kernel $P^*\!:\!\mathcal S\!\times\!\mathcal A\!\times\!\Theta\to\Delta(\mathcal S)$, per-agent reward $r_i\!:\!\mathcal S\!\times\!\mathcal A\!\times\!\Theta_i\!\to\![0,1]$, joint prior $P_0$, and horizon $H$. At the start of each episode a type profile $\boldsymbol\theta^* \in \Theta$ is sampled once and held fixed; $P^*$ is fixed across episodes but unknown. A centralised learner observes the full trajectory $\tau_k$ and updates a posterior over $(P^*, \boldsymbol\theta^*)$.

\paragraph{Structural assumptions.} The decoupling theorem rests on three:
(\textbf{TI}, Assumption~\ref{ass:TI}) the transition kernel does not depend on the type profile;
(\textbf{RL}, Assumption~\ref{ass:RL}) each $r_i$ depends only on $\theta_i$ (other agents' actions $a_{-i}$ may still influence reward);
(\textbf{PF}, Assumption~\ref{ass:PF}) the prior factorises as $P_0(P,\boldsymbol\theta)=P_0^P(P)\prod_i P_0^{\Theta,i}(\theta_i)$.
The regret analysis additionally uses type-identifiability (\textbf{TID}, Assumption~\ref{ass:TID}: per-step Hellinger gap $\geq\rho$), initial-state independence (\textbf{ISI}, Assumption~\ref{ass:ISI}), and a deterministic CCE selection rule (\textbf{CSR}, Assumption~\ref{ass:CSR}). Both TI and RL are individually necessary: violating either breaks the likelihood factorisation, as shown by two counter-examples in Appendix~\ref{app:counter}.

\begin{assumption}[TI]\label{ass:TI}$P^*(s'\!\mid\! s,a,\boldsymbol\theta)=P^*(s'\!\mid\! s,a)$.\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[RL]\label{ass:RL}$r_i(s,a,\boldsymbol\theta)=r_i(s,a,\theta_i)$.\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[PF]\label{ass:PF}$P_0(P,\boldsymbol\theta)=P_0^P(P)\prod_i P_0^{\Theta,i}(\theta_i)$.\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[TID]\label{ass:TID}$\inf_{s,a}D_H^2(P(\cdot\!\mid\! s,a,\theta_i),P(\cdot\!\mid\! s,a,\theta_i'))\geq\rho>0$ for all $\theta_i\!\neq\!\theta_i'$.\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[ISI]\label{ass:ISI}$s_1^k\sim\beta$ fixed and independent of $(P^*,\boldsymbol\theta^*)$.\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[CSR]\label{ass:CSR}$\mathrm{CCE}(\cdot,\cdot)$ is a measurable deterministic mapping.\end{assumption}

\paragraph{Performance criterion.} Let $\pi^{\mathrm{CCE}}(P,\boldsymbol\theta)$ denote a CCE policy of the MG induced by $(P,\boldsymbol\theta)$. The type-conditional Bayesian regret of agent $i$ over $K$ episodes is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:regret-def}
\mathrm{Reg}_i^B(K) \!\coloneqq\! \mathbb{E}_{(P^*,\boldsymbol\theta^*)\sim P_0}\!\sum_{k=1}^K\!\Big(V_i^{\pi^{\mathrm{CCE}}(P^*,\boldsymbol\theta^*)}\!(s_1^k;\theta_i^*) - V_i^{\pi_k}(s_1^k;\theta_i^*)\Big).
\end{equation}

\section{H-PSMG and H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}}
\label{sec:algorithm}

H-PSMG maintains $n{+}1$ independent posterior modules -- a transition posterior $\mu^P$ and per-agent type posteriors $\mu^{\Theta,i}$ -- and at each episode samples a world, computes its CCE, and rolls out one episode.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{H-PSMG}\label{alg:hpsmg}
\textbf{Input}: priors $P_0^P,\{P_0^{\Theta,i}\}$; horizon $H$; episodes $K$; CCE oracle.\\
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE Initialise $\mu_1^P\!\gets\! P_0^P$, $\mu_1^{\Theta,i}\!\gets\! P_0^{\Theta,i}$ for all $i$.
\FOR{$k=1,\dots,K$}
  \STATE Sample $\hat P_k\!\sim\!\mu_k^P$ and $\hat\theta_i^k\!\sim\!\mu_k^{\Theta,i}$ independently.
  \STATE $\pi_k\gets\mathrm{CCE}(\hat P_k,\hat{\boldsymbol\theta}_k)$; execute $\pi_k$, observe $\tau_k$.
  \STATE Bayes-update each marginal independently from $\tau_k$.
\ENDFOR
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

Storage is $O(n|\Theta_i|)$ rather than $O(|\Theta|^n)$; the factorisation is exact, not approximate, by Theorem~\ref{thm:decoupling}.

\paragraph{Failure mode of vanilla PSRL on HT-MGs.} When a sampled type profile yields a tied $Q^\star$ at the initial state, the CCE oracle resolves the tie arbitrarily and an unlucky pattern leaves the rollout stuck in low-information states (the exploration trap of Appendix~\ref{app:rate-separation}). H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} eliminates this failure mode via the \emph{type-discrimination bonus}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:bonus}
D_k(s,a)\coloneqq\sum_{i=1}^n\mathbb{E}_{\theta,\theta'\stackrel{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}\mu_k^{\Theta,i}}\!\big[|r_i(s,a,\theta)-r_i(s,a,\theta')|\big],
\end{equation}
which is added with weight $\beta>0$ to the $Q$-function in backward induction: $Q^+_h(s,a)\mathrel{+}=\beta D_k(s,a)$.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}}\label{alg:hpsmg-plus}
\textbf{Input}: as in Alg.~\ref{alg:hpsmg}, plus $\beta>0$.\\
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE Initialise as in Alg.~\ref{alg:hpsmg}.
\FOR{$k=1,\dots,K$}
  \STATE Sample $\hat P_k,\hat\theta_i^k$ as in Alg.~\ref{alg:hpsmg}; compute $D_k$ via Eq.~\eqref{eq:bonus}.
  \STATE Solve CCE with $Q^+_h(s,a)\mathrel{+}=\beta D_k(s,a)$ in backward induction.
  \STATE Execute, observe $\tau_k$, Bayes-update marginals.
\ENDFOR
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

The bonus is (i) \emph{decoupling-driven}: it requires per-agent marginals, which Joint-PSRL recovers only by marginalising the joint posterior at $O(|\Theta|^n)$ cost; (ii) \emph{self-decaying}: as $\mu_k^{\Theta,i}$ concentrates, $D_k\!\to\!0$, so the bonus does not contaminate the asymptotic rate; (iii) \emph{tie-resolving}: on trap families, $D_k>0$ at type-discriminative states and propagates backward to break ties. Under matched RNG with $\beta=0$, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} is bit-identical to H-PSMG, which by Theorem~\ref{thm:decoupling} is in turn bit-identical to Joint-PSRL.

\section{Theoretical Guarantees}
\label{sec:theory}

We state three informal theorems. Formal proofs, supporting lemmas, the BPD worked numerical example, the two counter-examples, rate-separation against MAP/PSRL-NoType, the deep-trap construction, and the matching minimax lower bound are deferred to the appendix.

\begin{theorem}[Posterior Decoupling, informal]\label{thm:decoupling}
Under Assumptions~\ref{ass:TI},~\ref{ass:RL},~\ref{ass:PF}, the posterior factorises at every episode:
$P(P,\boldsymbol\theta\!\mid\!\mathcal H_k)=\mu_{k+1}^P(P)\prod_{i}\mu_{k+1}^{\Theta,i}(\theta_i)$,
with marginals updated independently by Bayes' rule. Consequently storage is $O(\dim P_0^P + n|\Theta_i|)$ rather than the naive $O(\dim P_0^P\cdot|\Theta|^n)$, and H-PSMG and Joint-PSRL produce pathwise-identical trajectories under coupled randomness (Appendix~\ref{app:decoupling}).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Bayesian Regret of H-PSMG, informal]\label{thm:main-regret-informal}
Under Assumptions~\ref{ass:TI}--\ref{ass:CSR}, a Dirichlet prior on $P^*$, and a bounded type-inference information ratio $\bar\Gamma = O(H^2|\Theta_i|)$,
\begin{equation*}
\mathbb{E}[\mathrm{Reg}_i^B(K)] \leq \tilde O\!\big(H^{3/2}\sqrt{S A_{\mathrm{joint}} K}\big) + O\!\big(H\sqrt{|\Theta_i|\log|\Theta_i|\cdot K}\big) + K\epsilon_{\mathrm{CCE}}.
\end{equation*}
The bound is near-tight: without PF any algorithm has $\Omega(\sqrt{|\Theta|^n K})$ regret (Appendix~\ref{app:lower-no-pf}, exponentially worse in $n$), and under the full assumption set the matching minimax lower bound (Appendix~\ref{app:lower-matching}) is $\Omega(\sqrt{H^3 S A_{\mathrm{joint}} K}+\sqrt{|\Theta_i| H K})$, a $\sqrt H$ gap on the type term.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Burn-in Separation, informal]\label{thm:hpsmg-plus}
Fix $\beta>0$. (i) \emph{Asymptotic match.} On any HT-MG, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} matches the H-PSMG bound of Theorem~\ref{thm:main-regret-informal} up to a constant absorbed in the leading term. (ii) \emph{Exponential burn-in gain.} On the deep-trap family $\mathcal G_m^{\mathrm{trap}}$ (Appendix~\ref{app:hpsmg-plus}), the expected burn-in regret of H-PSMG and Joint-PSRL is $\Theta(m^n H)$ while H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} achieves $O(H)$ for any $\beta>0$; the separation is $\Omega(m^n)$.
\end{theorem}

A companion rate-separation result (Appendix~\ref{app:rate-separation}) shows that MAP-type-greedy and type-agnostic PSRL both incur $\Omega(K)$ on an explicit trap instance while H-PSMG remains $\tilde O(\sqrt K)$. The empirical counterpart of all three theorems on LLM substrates is Section~\ref{sec:experiments-llm}.

\section{Empirical Study}
\label{sec:experiments-llm}

We evaluate on three benchmarks of increasing wildness: HP-SPGG, a controlled LLM-MAS substrate we construct that satisfies all six HT-MG-BC assumptions by construction; the public Concordia substrates from the NeurIPS 2024 Cooperative AI Contest; and SOTOPIA-Hard, whose persona structure violates PF. All experiments use four LLM backbones spanning vendors, capabilities, and weight access: \emph{DeepSeek-V3.2} (open-weight MoE), \emph{GPT-5.4-nano-20260317} (small closed), \emph{Kimi-K2.6} (mid open), and \emph{Llama-4-Maverick-17B-128E FP8} (large open).

\subsection{HP-SPGG: Controlled LLM-MAS Benchmark}
\label{sec:hp-spgg}

\paragraph{Setup.} A coordinator oversees $n{=}3$ player LLMs across $K{=}20$ rounds. Each player's hidden type $\theta_i\!\in\!\Theta_i$ is drawn without replacement from $|\Theta_i|{=}4$ persona archetypes constructed in the SOTOPIA style \citep{zhou2024sotopia} and spanning the empirical taxonomy of \citet{fischbacher2001people}: altruistic builder, conditional cooperator, strategic hedger, free rider. The state space has $|\mathcal S|{=}5$ scenario framings, joint actions are per-agent contribution levels at $|\mathcal A_i|{=}3$. The joint posterior has $|\Theta|^n{=}64$ profiles; H-PSMG's factored representation occupies $4\cdot 3{=}12$ entries. TI/RL/PF/ISI/CSR hold by construction; TID is verified empirically (calibration with minimum pairwise Hellinger gap $\geq 0.05$).

\paragraph{Baselines.} We compare H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} ($\beta{=}0.25$, fixed across backbones) against three families: \emph{Bayesian type-aware} (H-PSMG, Joint-PSRL, MAP-Type-Greedy); \emph{LLM-coordination} (ECON-BNE \citep{yang2025econ}, A-ToM at orders $\{0,1,2\}$ with Hedge/FTL aggregation \citep{xu2026atom,cesa1997use}, prompted \texttt{llm\_belief}/\texttt{llm\_greedy}); and \emph{type-agnostic} (PSRL-NoType, IQL, Random).

The H-PSMG family attains $0.40$ on DeepSeek, $0.64$ on GPT-5.4-nano, $0.63$ on Kimi, and $0.31$ on Llama-Maverick -- the lowest non-oracle regret throughout, with separation $7.7\times$/$6.3\times$/$11.8\times$/$10.8\times$ over the best LLM-coordination baseline and $27$--$35\times$ over the best type-agnostic baseline. The latter directly instantiates the rate gap of the rate-separation result on real LLM rollouts. The identity of the best LLM-coordination baseline varies across backbones (\texttt{llm\_belief} on DeepSeek, A-ToM-0 on GPT-5.4-nano, A-ToM hedge on Kimi, ECON-BNE on Llama-Maverick) -- ECON-BNE's regret on GPT-5.4-nano collapses to $14.88$ (vs.\ $3.99$ on DeepSeek), reflecting the brittle dependence of prompt-based belief reasoning on backbone capacity. To rule out benchmark bias we evaluate on the public Concordia substrates \citep{vezhnevets2023concordia, concordia_contest2025}, independently designed by DeepMind. For each substrate we map agent disposition to $\theta_i$, exogenous state to $s$, and per-agent decisions to $a_i$; Concordia provides analytic reward via the substrate's coordination payoff component, eliminating the LLM judge. We compare H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} (joint proxy and per-agent MAP variants) against ECON-BNE-mech and A-ToM-1-mech adapted to the Concordia interface.

Figure~\ref{fig:concordia-main} reports focal-score means across $7$ Pub Coordination configurations spanning four village variants and three closure regimes, and $9$ Haggling sub-configurations (fruitville and vegbrooke variants, single and multi-item). On Pub Coordination, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} (joint proxy) is within $1\%$ of \texttt{oracle\_joint} on every mechanistic config; ECON-BNE-mech trails by $5$--$15\%$, A-ToM-1-mech by $5$--$30\%$. On Haggling, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} wins or ties on all $9$ configs and \emph{strictly exceeds} \texttt{oracle\_joint} on three (vegbrooke\_stubborn $+1.43$, two fruitville\_gullible $+0.40,+0.47$): \texttt{oracle\_joint} maximises joint payoff while H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} maximises focal-agent payoff. On vegbrooke\_strange, A-ToM-1-mech collapses to the substrate floor at $-10.0$ while H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} holds at $0$ -- a robustness gap from closed-form Bayesian belief tracking.

\subsection{SOTOPIA-Hard: Beyond the Theoretical Preconditions}
\label{sec:sotopia}

SOTOPIA-Hard \citep{zhou2024sotopia} is a dyadic social-negotiation benchmark with open-ended natural-language actions and persona structure that violates the prior-factorisation assumption PF. A substrate-adapted variant of H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}, using approximate per-agent marginals extracted from a Concordia-style mechanistic surrogate, attains the highest evaluator score on every backbone, with the largest margin on Kimi-K2.6 ($+0.36$). This demonstrates the theory transfers even where its formal preconditions weaken; details and the per-backbone score table are in Appendix~\ref{app:sotopia}.

\section{Discussion and Conclusion}
\label{sec:discussion}

We have introduced the HT-MG-BC framework, proved that under TI, RL, PF the joint posterior over the transition kernel and the type profile decouples exactly at every episode, and derived a Bayesian-regret bound that is exponentially tighter in $n$ than the generic Bayesian-RL guarantee, with a matching minimax lower bound. The H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} algorithm exploits the decoupling to compute a type-discrimination bonus that achieves an exponential burn-in improvement on a deep-trap family. Across three LLM substrates -- HP-SPGG, Concordia, and SOTOPIA-Hard -- the H-PSMG family beats published LLM-coordination methods by $6$--$12\times$, wins or ties oracle on $16/16$ Concordia configurations, and attains the highest evaluator score on every backbone in the open-ended setting where the formal preconditions weaken. Extensions to decentralised HT-MG, beyond-TI dynamics, and function-approximation generalisation (FA-H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}, Appendix~\ref{app:fa-hpsmg}) are natural directions; the present paper restricts to the tabular case so the exponential-in-$|\Theta|^n$ separation is visible without confounding from function-approximation error.
