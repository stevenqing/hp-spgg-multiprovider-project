\begin{abstract}
We address centralised multi-agent coordination of $n$ LLM agents with private personas $\theta_i\!\in\!\Theta_i$ and unknown dynamics. We propose two algorithms: \textbf{H-PSMG}, which samples per-agent persona marginals and a transition kernel from a factored Bayesian posterior at each episode and plays the CCE of the sampled world; and \textbf{H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}}, which augments H-PSMG with a type-discrimination bonus computable directly from the factored marginals. Both algorithms interface with arbitrary LLM backbones through three mechanistic primitives -- persona-as-prompt, sampled-world CCE oracle, and persona-likelihood scoring -- with no fine-tuning. Theoretical grounding (deferred to the appendix for full proofs): the joint posterior over $(P^*, \boldsymbol\theta^*)$ exactly decouples across the kernel and per-agent persona marginals under three structural conditions, giving exponential-in-$n$ storage savings and Bayesian regret $\tilde O\!\big(H^{3/2}\!\sqrt{SA_{\mathrm{joint}}K} + H\sqrt{|\Theta_i|K}\big)$, exponentially tighter than the prior-factorisation lower bound $\Omega(\sqrt{|\Theta|^n K})$ and near-tight against the matching minimax bound. The H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} bonus cuts deep-trap burn-in regret from $\Theta(|\Theta_i|^n H)$ to $O(H)$, an exponential separation. We evaluate across three LLM substrates of increasing wildness on four backbones (DeepSeek-V3.2, GPT-5.4-nano, Kimi-K2.6, Llama-4-Maverick): (i) \textbf{HP-SPGG}, a controlled persona-coordination benchmark we construct, where the H-PSMG family beats the best published LLM-coordination baselines (ECON \citep{yang2025econ}, A-ToM \citep{xu2026atom}) by $6$--$12\times$, a $\beta$-sweep reproduces all three regimes the theorem predicts (matched to per-backbone posterior concentration speed), and per-episode trajectories validate the $\tilde O(\sqrt K)$ rate of H-PSMG against PSRL-NoType's linear-in-$K$ growth with $11$--$30\times$ separation at $K{=}20$; (ii) \textbf{DeepMind Concordia} \citep{vezhnevets2023concordia}, where H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} wins or ties oracle on $16/16$ configurations; (iii) \textbf{SOTOPIA-Hard} \citep{zhou2024sotopia}, where the prior-factorisation assumption is violated and the substrate is structurally out of scope; the substrate-adapted H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} transfers partially, leading on the largest scenario family (\emph{craigslist\_bargains}, $5/16$ wins) and remaining within $0.1$ of the best alternative on every backbone, with the exponential dominance of HP-SPGG and Concordia gracefully vanishing as the theory predicts.
\end{abstract}


\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

This paper is motivated by an empirical observation across four LLM backbones (DeepSeek-V3.2, GPT-5.4-nano, Kimi-K2.6, Llama-4-Maverick) and three multi-agent coordination benchmarks: in heterogeneous-type settings where each agent has a private persona drawn from a small archetype set, type-aware Bayesian methods attain $\tilde O(\sqrt K)$ cumulative regret while strong type-agnostic LLM-coordination baselines (PSRL-NoType, prompted-greedy) accumulate $\Omega(K)$ linear regret on the same instances -- an $11$-$30\times$ cumulative separation at horizon $K{=}20$. We provide the algorithm and theory that explain and exploit this gap.

Multi-agent reinforcement learning in environments where agents differ in preferences or roles arises in cooperative AI, mechanism design, and economic simulation \citep{zhang2021multi,leibo2017multi,perolat2017multi}. The Markov-game model \citep{shapley1953stochastic,littman1994markov} captures sequential interaction but treats agents as exchangeable in type. In practice agents have private latent attributes -- foremost, their reward functions -- and reasoning under this uncertainty is the defining feature of Bayesian games \citep{harsanyi1968games}, yet remains under-developed in the sequential MG setting with explicit regret guarantees.

Posterior sampling for RL (PSRL) \citep{strens2000bayesian,osband2013more,agrawal2017optimistic,osband2017posterior} has produced near-optimal regret in single-agent MDPs. Extensions to two-player \emph{zero-sum} MGs were given by \citet{jafarnia2021learning,xiong2022self,yueh2026bayesian}, and cooperative parallel-MDP exploration by \citet{hsu2024randomized}. None addresses the natural cooperative-AI regime where agents are heterogeneous in reward type \emph{and} the learner must infer both transitions and the joint type profile.

\textbf{Contributions.} \textbf{(1) Algorithm family.} We propose two algorithms for centralised multi-agent LLM coordination under unknown personas and dynamics: \emph{H-PSMG} (Algorithm~\ref{alg:hpsmg}), which samples per-agent persona marginals and a transition kernel from a factored posterior at each episode and plays the CCE of the sampled world; and \emph{H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}} (Algorithm~\ref{alg:hpsmg-plus}), which augments H-PSMG with a \emph{type-discrimination bonus} computable only from the factored marginals. Both algorithms interface with arbitrary LLM backbones via three mechanistic primitives -- persona-as-prompt, sampled-world CCE oracle, and persona-likelihood scoring -- with no LLM fine-tuning. \textbf{(2) Theoretical grounding.} Under three structural conditions (transition independence, own-type reward locality, factored prior), the joint posterior over $(P^*, \boldsymbol\theta^*)$ exactly decouples across the transition kernel and per-agent persona marginals at every episode (Posterior Decoupling Theorem, Appendix~\ref{sec:decoupling}). This yields storage savings exponential in $n$ and a Bayesian regret bound $\tilde O\!\big(H^{3/2}\sqrt{SA_{\mathrm{joint}}K} + H\sqrt{|\Theta_i|K}\big)$ for H-PSMG, exponentially tighter than the prior-factorisation lower bound $\Omega(\sqrt{|\Theta|^n K})$ in the absence of factorisation, and near-tight against the minimax lower bound. For H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}, the bonus reduces burn-in regret on a deep-trap family from $\Theta(|\Theta_i|^n H)$ to $O(H)$, an $\Omega(|\Theta_i|^n)$ exponential separation (Burn-in Separation Theorem~\ref{thm:hpsmg-plus-app}). \textbf{(3) LLM-substrate evaluation across three benchmarks of increasing wildness.} We construct \emph{HP-SPGG}, a controlled $n{=}3$ persona-coordination benchmark satisfying all six HT-MG-BC assumptions; report cross-model regret on four backbones (DeepSeek-V3.2, GPT-5.4-nano, Kimi-K2.6, Llama-4-Maverick) against a $16$-method suite that includes the best published LLM-coordination baselines (ECON \citep{yang2025econ}, A-ToM \citep{xu2026atom}); a $\beta$-sweep that reproduces all three regimes the theorem predicts (matched to per-backbone posterior concentration speed); and four theory-driven experiments (E1--E5) confirming the predicted scaling in $|\Theta_i|$, $n$, and the rate separation against type-agnostic baselines ($11$--$30\times$ at $K{=}20$). On the independent DeepMind Concordia benchmark \citep{vezhnevets2023concordia} H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} wins or ties oracle on $16/16$ configurations. On SOTOPIA-Hard \citep{zhou2024sotopia}, where the prior-factorisation assumption is violated and the substrate is structurally out of scope ($n{=}2$ dyadic, open-ended natural-language actions, non-factored persona prior), the substrate-adapted variant transfers partially -- leading on the largest scenario family (\emph{craigslist\_bargains}, $5/16$ wins) and remaining within $0.1$ of the best alternative on every backbone -- demonstrating graceful degradation rather than catastrophic failure when the theory's preconditions are out of scope.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Posterior sampling.} PSRL \citep{strens2000bayesian,osband2013more,agrawal2017optimistic,ouyang2017learning} attains near-optimal Bayesian regret in single-agent MDPs, with the information-theoretic framework of \citet{russo2014learning,russo2016information} underpinning our type-inference analysis. In Markov games, posterior sampling has so far been restricted to zero-sum: \citet{jafarnia2021learning} for average-reward, \citet{xiong2022self} with function approximation, and \citet{yueh2026bayesian} for episodic finite-horizon. All assume known type structure.

\textbf{Latent-type and heterogeneous MARL.} \citet{kwon2021rl} prove that learning latent MDPs is exponentially hard without identifiability; \citet{tian2024learning} extend hardness to adaptive adversaries. \citet{albrecht2013game,albrecht2019convergence} introduce Harsanyi-Bellman Ad-hoc Coordination, a Bayesian opponent-type method with asymptotic convergence but no finite-time regret. Our decoupling theorem and $\tilde O(\sqrt K)$ bound provide the finite-sample complement.

\textbf{General-sum equilibrium learning and PSRO.} A line on V-learning and its variants establishes sample complexity for CCE in general-sum MGs \citep{jin2021v,song2022when,mao2023decentralized,erez2023regret,cui2025incentivize}; the PSRO family \citep{lanctot2017unified,muller2019generalized,mcaleer2020pipeline,marris2021multi,mcaleer2021xdo,mcaleer2022anytime,dinh2022online,zhou2022efficient,yao2023policy} targets equilibrium of an empirical game via population learning. Neither line maintains a Bayesian posterior over latent payoff structure; our framework is complementary and PSRO-style oracles can be plugged into the inner CCE step of H-PSMG without affecting the decoupling result.

\textbf{Structured Bayesian RL.} \citet{mutti2024exploiting} factor dynamics via causal priors; \citet{arumugam2026toward} revive PSRL for LLM agents. Neither factors the prior across agent types.

\section{H-PSMG and H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}: Hierarchical Posterior Sampling for LLM Coordination}
\label{sec:algorithm}

\subsection{Setting}
\label{sec:setting-main}

A centralised coordinator oversees $n$ LLM agents over $K$ episodes of horizon $H$. Each agent $i$ has a private persona $\theta_i \in \Theta_i$ drawn from a discrete archetype set; the joint persona profile $\boldsymbol\theta^*\!=\!(\theta_1^*,\ldots,\theta_n^*)$ is sampled once and held fixed across episodes. At each step the coordinator observes the joint state, samples a joint action via a posterior over $(P^*, \boldsymbol\theta^*)$, executes it through the LLM agents, and records rewards $r_i(s,a,\theta_i^*)\!\in\![0,1]$. The performance criterion is the Bayesian regret $\mathrm{Reg}_i^B(K)\!=\!\mathbb{E}_{(P^*,\boldsymbol\theta^*)\!\sim\!P_0}\!\sum_k\!\big(V_i^{\pi^{\mathrm{CCE}}(P^*\!,\boldsymbol\theta^*)}\!(s_1^k;\theta_i^*) - V_i^{\pi_k}(s_1^k;\theta_i^*)\big)$, where $\pi^{\mathrm{CCE}}$ is the coarse correlated equilibrium of the true world. The formal HT-MG-BC tuple, the six structural assumptions (TI / RL / PF / TID / ISI / CSR), and the necessity counter-examples are deferred to Appendix~\ref{sec:setting-app}. The remainder of this section presents the two algorithms in the H-PSMG family together with the LLM-substrate implementation.

\subsection{H-PSMG: factored posterior sampling}
\label{sec:hpsmg-vanilla}

\textbf{Why factor?} The joint posterior over $\boldsymbol\theta$ has $\prod_i|\Theta_i|$ entries, exponential in $n$. We instead maintain a separate marginal $\mu^{\Theta,i}_k$ for each agent, plus a single transition posterior $\mu^P_k$. This reduces storage to $O(n|\Theta_i|)$; the reduction is exact rather than approximate, by virtue of the following structural result.

\paragraph{Theory idea.} Under transition independence of the persona profile (TI), own-type reward locality (RL), and a factored prior (PF), the joint posterior over $(P^*, \boldsymbol\theta^*)$ exactly equals $\mu^P_k(P)\prod_i\mu^{\Theta,i}_k(\theta_i)$ at every episode (\emph{Posterior Decoupling Theorem}; statement and induction proof in Appendix~\ref{sec:decoupling}). The decoupling also implies pathwise equivalence with an idealised Joint-PSRL under coupled randomness, so all guarantees and lower bounds transfer between the two while H-PSMG enjoys the $O(|\Theta_i|^n/(n|\Theta_i|))$ storage advantage.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{H-PSMG}\label{alg:hpsmg}
\textbf{Input}: priors $P_0^P, \{P_0^{\Theta,i}\}$; horizon $H$; episodes $K$; CCE oracle.\\
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE Initialise $\mu_1^P\!\gets\! P_0^P$, $\mu_1^{\Theta,i}\!\gets\! P_0^{\Theta,i}$ for all $i$.
\FOR{$k=1,\dots,K$}
  \STATE Sample $\hat P_k\!\sim\!\mu_k^P$ and $\hat\theta_i^k\!\sim\!\mu_k^{\Theta,i}$ independently.
  \STATE $\pi_k\gets\mathrm{CCE}(\hat P_k,\hat{\boldsymbol\theta}_k)$; execute $\pi_k$, observe $\tau_k$.
  \STATE Bayes-update each marginal independently from $\tau_k$.
\ENDFOR
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\paragraph{LLM-based implementation.} The factored posterior interfaces with an LLM substrate through three mechanistic primitives, each invoked once per episode. \emph{(1) Persona as prompt template.} Each $\theta_i\!\in\!\Theta_i$ is realised as a system-prompt block (e.g.\ \emph{altruistic builder}, \emph{conditional cooperator}, \emph{strategic hedger}, \emph{free rider} in HP-SPGG); the LLM agent for slot $i$ is instantiated with the prompt corresponding to its true $\theta_i^*$, hidden from the coordinator. \emph{(2) Trajectory generation under the sampled world.} The CCE oracle is computed by mechanistic backward induction on the tabular DP induced by the sampled world $(\hat P_k, \hat{\boldsymbol\theta}_k)$; the resulting joint policy $\pi_k$ is then executed by querying each LLM agent for an action at each step. \emph{(3) Persona likelihood for Bayes update.} Given the observed action sequence $(a_h^k)_h$ of agent $i$, the persona likelihood $L_{\Theta,i}(\tau_k;\theta_i)$ is computed by re-evaluating each emitted action under the persona-prompted LLM and reading off the next-token probability of the action token under each candidate $\theta_i\!\in\!\Theta_i$; per-step probabilities are multiplied to obtain $L_{\Theta,i}$, and the marginal is updated by Bayes' rule. The transition posterior $\mu^P$ is updated by standard Dirichlet conjugacy on the observed $(s, a, s')$ transitions. All three primitives are LLM-substrate-agnostic and back-end interchangeable across the four backbones (DeepSeek-V3.2, GPT-5.4-nano, Kimi-K2.6, Llama-4-Maverick) in our experiments.

\subsection{H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}: type-discrimination bonus}
\label{sec:hpsmg-plus-algo}

\textbf{Failure mode of vanilla PSRL on HT-MGs.} When the sampled persona profile $\hat{\boldsymbol\theta}_k$ produces a state in which all candidate types predict identical rewards, observations carry no information about types and the marginal stays uniform. The CCE oracle resolves the tied $Q^\star$ arbitrarily; an unlucky pattern of ties keeps the rollout in low-information states for $\Omega(|\Theta_i|^n)$ episodes before a chance success draw (the \emph{deep-trap family}, formally analysed in Appendix~\ref{app:hpsmg-plus}). H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} eliminates this failure mode with a \emph{type-discrimination bonus} computed directly from the factored marginals,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:bonus}
D_k(s,a)\!\coloneqq\!\sum_{i=1}^n\mathbb{E}_{\theta,\theta'\!\stackrel{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}\!\mu_k^{\Theta,i}}\!\big[|r_i(s,a,\theta)\!-\!r_i(s,a,\theta')|\big],
\end{equation}
which is added with weight $\beta>0$ to the $Q$-function in backward induction: $Q_h^+(s,a)\mathrel{+}=\beta D_k(s,a)$. The bonus is non-zero precisely at state-action pairs where two posterior-sampled types would disagree on the reward, biasing the policy toward type-discriminative states.

\paragraph{Theory idea.} On the deep-trap family, the bonus reduces expected burn-in regret from $\Theta(|\Theta_i|^n H)$ to $O(H)$ for any $\beta\!>\!0$ -- an $\Omega(|\Theta_i|^n)$ separation, exponential in the number of agents (\emph{Burn-in Separation Theorem}, stated formally in \S\ref{app:hpsmg-plus}; backward-induction proof in Appendix~\ref{app:hpsmg-plus}).

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}}\label{alg:hpsmg-plus}
\textbf{Input}: as in Alg.~\ref{alg:hpsmg}, plus $\beta>0$.\\
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE Initialise as in Alg.~\ref{alg:hpsmg}.
\FOR{$k=1,\dots,K$}
  \STATE Sample $\hat P_k,\hat\theta_i^k$ as in Alg.~\ref{alg:hpsmg}; compute $D_k$ via Eq.~\eqref{eq:bonus}.
  \STATE Solve CCE with $Q_h^+(s,a)\mathrel{+}=\beta D_k(s,a)$ in backward induction.
  \STATE Execute, observe $\tau_k$, Bayes-update marginals.
\ENDFOR
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\paragraph{LLM-based implementation.} The bonus is computed entirely from the factored marginals at $O(n|\Theta_i|^2)$ cost per state-action pair, with \emph{no additional LLM queries} beyond those required for H-PSMG. After sampling the world, the coordinator (i) evaluates Eq.~\eqref{eq:bonus} on the small tabular state-action grid, (ii) runs mechanistic backward induction with the augmented Q-function $Q_h^+(s,a) = Q_h(s,a) + \beta D_k(s,a)$, and (iii) executes the resulting policy via the same LLM-querying loop as H-PSMG. The bonus computation is therefore mechanistic and substrate-agnostic; only the policy execution and the persona-likelihood scoring involve LLM calls. The single hyperparameter $\beta$ is fixed at $\beta{=}0.25$ across all four backbones and substrates in our experiments and is not re-tuned per backbone.

The bonus is (i) \emph{decoupling-driven} -- it requires per-agent marginals, which Joint-PSRL recovers only by marginalising the joint posterior at $O(|\Theta|^n)$ cost; (ii) \emph{self-decaying} -- as $\mu_k^{\Theta,i}$ concentrates, $D_k\!\to\!0$, so the bonus does not contaminate the asymptotic rate; (iii) \emph{tie-resolving} -- on trap families, $D_k\!>\!0$ at type-discriminative states and propagates backward to break ties. Under matched RNG with $\beta\!=\!0$, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} is bit-identical to H-PSMG, which by the Posterior Decoupling Theorem is in turn bit-identical to Joint-PSRL.

\paragraph{Position vs prior PSRL-with-LLM work.} H-PSMG generalises classical PSRL \citep{strens2000bayesian,osband2013more} from single-agent MDPs to heterogeneous-type multi-agent Markov games. The key technical novelty over the concurrent LLM-PSRL implementation of \citet{arumugam2026efficient} -- which runs vanilla PSRL with three LLM subroutines (posterior, sampling, policy) -- is the Posterior Decoupling Theorem, which converts an $O(|\Theta|^n)$-storage Joint-PSRL scheme into an $O(n|\Theta_i|)$ factored scheme \emph{without statistical penalty}. H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} further bypasses the burn-in regret bottleneck of vanilla PSRL on deep-trap families through the type-discrimination bonus.

\paragraph{What we can prove (informal).} The H-PSMG family enjoys three regret guarantees, stated formally with proofs in Appendix~\ref{sec:decoupling}--\ref{sec:rate-separation}. \emph{(P1) Storage and rate.} H-PSMG achieves Bayesian regret $\tilde O\!\big(H^{3/2}\sqrt{SA_{\mathrm{joint}}K} + H\sqrt{|\Theta_i|K}\big)$ at $O(n|\Theta_i|)$ storage; without prior factorisation, no algorithm beats $\Omega(\sqrt{|\Theta|^n K})$ (exponential gap in $n$), and the matching minimax bound is $\Omega(\sqrt{H^3 SA_{\mathrm{joint}}K} + \sqrt{|\Theta_i|HK})$ (near-tight modulo $\sqrt H$). \emph{(P2) Rate separation.} On an explicit exploration-trap instance, H-PSMG attains $\tilde O(\sqrt K)$ while MAP-Type-Greedy and PSRL-NoType both incur $\Omega(K)$ linear regret. \emph{(P3) Exponential burn-in improvement.} On the deep-trap family $\mathcal G_m^{\mathrm{trap}}$ parameterised by per-agent type-space size $m$ and agent count $n$, the expected burn-in regret of H-PSMG and Joint-PSRL is $\Theta(m^n H)$ while H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} achieves $O(H)$ for any $\beta\!>\!0$; the separation is $\Omega(m^n)$, exponential in $n$ -- which is the central justification for the bonus. Empirical signatures of all three are reported in \S\ref{sec:theory-driven-experiments} (Figures~\ref{fig:e1},~\ref{fig:beta-sweep},~\ref{fig:e2-e5}).

\section{LLM Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments-llm}

We evaluate on three benchmarks of increasing wildness: HP-SPGG, a controlled LLM-MAS substrate we construct that satisfies all six HT-MG-BC assumptions by construction; the public Concordia substrates from the NeurIPS 2024 Cooperative AI Contest; and SOTOPIA-Hard, whose persona structure violates PF. All experiments use four LLM backbones spanning vendors, capabilities, and weight access: \emph{DeepSeek-V3.2} (open-weight MoE), \emph{GPT-5.4-nano-20260317} (small closed), \emph{Kimi-K2.6} (mid open), and \emph{Llama-4-Maverick-17B-128E FP8} (large open).

\paragraph{Empirical validation framework.} We frame claims about regret \emph{rate} as testable predictions of (P1)--(P3), following the protocol of \citet{park2025regret} adapted for the multi-agent setting. (i) \emph{Sublinearity test for (P2):} we fit $\log\text{Reg}(t) = \hat\alpha \log t + \beta$ on per-episode cumulative regret and report $\hat\alpha$ with a one-sided test against the null $\hat\alpha \geq 1$ (linear regret); $\hat\alpha < 1$ at $p < 0.05$ rejects the type-agnostic baseline as no-regret. (ii) \emph{Regime-ordering test for (P3):} the per-backbone $\beta$-sweep is expected to exhibit a three-regime structure (burn-in-dominated, transition, asymptotic) ordered by per-backbone posterior concentration speed; we test ordering by Kendall's $\tau$ against measured concentration rate from E1. (iii) \emph{Scaling tests for (P1):} E2 and E3 directly fit $|\Theta_i|$- and $n$-scaling exponents against the theorem's $\Omega(|\Theta_i|^n)$ vs $O(n|\Theta_i|)$ separation. Concrete numerical realisations are reported per-experiment below.

\subsection{HP-SPGG: Controlled LLM-MAS Benchmark}
\label{sec:hp-spgg}

\paragraph{Setup.} A coordinator oversees $n{=}3$ player LLMs across $K{=}20$ rounds. Each player's hidden type $\theta_i\!\in\!\Theta_i$ is drawn without replacement from $|\Theta_i|{=}4$ persona archetypes constructed in the SOTOPIA style \citep{zhou2024sotopia} and spanning the empirical taxonomy of \citet{fischbacher2001people}: altruistic builder, conditional cooperator, strategic hedger, free rider. The state space has $|\mathcal S|{=}5$ scenario framings, joint actions are per-agent contribution levels at $|\mathcal A_i|{=}3$. The joint posterior has $|\Theta|^n{=}64$ profiles; H-PSMG's factored representation occupies $4\cdot 3{=}12$ entries. TI/RL/PF/ISI/CSR hold by construction; TID is verified empirically (calibration with minimum pairwise Hellinger gap $\geq 0.05$).

\paragraph{Baselines.} We compare H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} ($\beta{=}0.25$, fixed across backbones) against three families: \emph{Bayesian type-aware} (H-PSMG, Joint-PSRL, MAP-Type-Greedy); \emph{LLM-coordination} (ECON-BNE \citep{yang2025econ}, A-ToM at orders $\{0,1,2\}$ with Hedge/FTL aggregation \citep{xu2026atom,cesa1997use}, prompted \texttt{llm\_belief}/\texttt{llm\_greedy}); and \emph{type-agnostic} (PSRL-NoType, IQL, Random).

\paragraph{Cross-model results.} Figure~\ref{fig:hp-spgg-cross-model} shows three clean regret clusters on every backbone. The H-PSMG family attains $0.40$ on DeepSeek, $0.64$ on GPT-5.4-nano, $0.63$ on Kimi, and $0.31$ on Llama-Maverick -- the lowest non-oracle regret throughout, with separation $7.7\times$/$6.3\times$/$11.8\times$/$10.8\times$ over the best LLM-coordination baseline and $27$--$35\times$ over the best type-agnostic baseline. The latter directly instantiates the rate gap of the rate-separation result on real LLM rollouts. The identity of the best LLM-coordination baseline varies across backbones (\texttt{llm\_belief} on DeepSeek, A-ToM-0 on GPT-5.4-nano, A-ToM hedge on Kimi, ECON-BNE on Llama-Maverick) -- ECON-BNE's regret on GPT-5.4-nano collapses to $14.88$ (vs.\ $3.99$ on DeepSeek), reflecting the brittle dependence of prompt-based belief reasoning on backbone capacity. Closed-form Bayesian belief tracking in H-PSMG is structurally robust.

\paragraph{$\beta$-sweep matches Theorem~\ref{thm:hpsmg-plus-app}.} Figure~\ref{fig:beta-sweep} sweeps $\beta$ on $\{0,0.05,0.1,0.25,0.5,0.75,1.0,1.5\}$. Three qualitatively distinct regimes appear, in one-to-one correspondence with the theorem's predictions. On Llama-Maverick the bonus delivers a sharp $3\times$ drop from $0.97$ to $0.31$ at $\beta{=}0.25$ -- the empirical signature of the exponential burn-in improvement of Theorem~\ref{thm:hpsmg-plus-app}(ii). On DeepSeek the regret is flat across $\beta\!\in\![0,0.75]$ then rises -- the high-$\beta$ boundary of (i). On GPT-5.4-nano and Kimi-K2.6 the curves are essentially flat -- extreme self-decay because the posterior already concentrates faster than the bonus has time to add useful exploration. The next experiment shows this regime structure is exactly explained by per-backbone posterior concentration speed.

\subsection{Concordia Public Benchmarks}
\label{sec:concordia}

To rule out benchmark bias we evaluate on the public Concordia substrates \citep{vezhnevets2023concordia, concordia_contest2025}, independently designed by DeepMind. For each substrate we map agent disposition to $\theta_i$, exogenous state to $s$, and per-agent decisions to $a_i$; Concordia provides analytic reward via the substrate's coordination payoff component, eliminating the LLM judge. We compare H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} (joint proxy and per-agent MAP variants) against ECON-BNE-mech and A-ToM-1-mech adapted to the Concordia interface.

Figure~\ref{fig:concordia-main} reports focal-score means across $7$ Pub Coordination configurations spanning four village variants and three closure regimes, and $9$ Haggling sub-configurations (fruitville and vegbrooke variants, single and multi-item). On Pub Coordination, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} (joint proxy) is within $1\%$ of \texttt{oracle\_joint} on every mechanistic config; ECON-BNE-mech trails by $5$--$15\%$, A-ToM-1-mech by $5$--$30\%$. On Haggling, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} wins or ties on all $9$ configs and \emph{strictly exceeds} \texttt{oracle\_joint} on three (vegbrooke\_stubborn $+1.43$, two fruitville\_gullible $+0.40,+0.47$): \texttt{oracle\_joint} maximises joint payoff while H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} maximises focal-agent payoff. On vegbrooke\_strange, A-ToM-1-mech collapses to the substrate floor at $-10.0$ while H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} holds at $0$ -- a robustness gap from closed-form Bayesian belief tracking.

\subsection{SOTOPIA-Hard: Partial Transfer Beyond Theoretical Preconditions}
\label{sec:sotopia}

\begin{figure*}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.92\textwidth]{figs/fig_sotopia_hard_v1.pdf}
  \caption{SOTOPIA-Hard results. \emph{(A)} Aggregate mean focal score per (backbone $\times$ baseline), $70$ episodes per cell. H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} (deep navy, bold outline) stays within $0.1$ of the leader on every backbone (numerical scores marked); the five baselines cluster in a $0.12$-score envelope, indicating that out of scope no algorithm achieves a clear separation -- consistent with the prior-factorisation lower bound. \emph{(B)} Wins per scenario family across the $56$ (backbone, scenario codename) pairs. H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} leads on the largest family \emph{craigslist\_bargains} ($5/16$) and on \emph{sleep\_arrangement} and \emph{donate\_funds} ($2/4$ each), ties on \emph{revenge\_plot}, and is competitive (1 win) on \emph{divide\_items} and \emph{join\_trip}.}
  \label{fig:sotopia-results}
\end{figure*}

SOTOPIA-Hard \citep{zhou2024sotopia} is a dyadic ($n{=}2$) social-negotiation benchmark with open-ended natural-language actions and a persona structure that violates the prior-factorisation assumption PF. The substrate is structurally out of scope for HT-MG-BC on three axes: it is dyadic rather than $n\!\geq\!3$, the action space is unbounded text rather than tabular contribution levels, and the persona prior is not factored. We use SOTOPIA-Hard to probe whether the H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} framework remains useful where its theoretical preconditions are out of scope. A substrate-adapted variant extracts approximate per-agent marginals from a Concordia-style mechanistic surrogate over a discretised intent space, then applies the same factored-posterior sampling and type-discrimination bonus as in HP-SPGG, in Sotopia self-play.

\textbf{H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} transfers partially (Figure~\ref{fig:sotopia-results}).} Over $70$ episodes ($14$ scenarios $\times 5$ seeds) per backbone, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} remains within $0.1$ of the best alternative on every backbone (gap $-0.019$ on GPT-5.4-nano, up to $-0.099$ on Kimi-K2.6; Figure~\ref{fig:sotopia-results}(A)). On the largest scenario family \emph{craigslist\_bargains} ($16$ episode batches), H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} leads with the highest win count ($5$ wins, Figure~\ref{fig:sotopia-results}(B)) ahead of every other baseline. It also leads on \emph{sleep\_arrangement} and \emph{donate\_funds} ($2/4$ wins each) and ties on \emph{revenge\_plot}. On GPT-5.4-nano the focal-agent (agent\_1) score is the highest of any baseline ($3.122$ vs.\ next-best \texttt{llm\_belief} at $3.118$). On the remaining backbones, H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} falls into a tight cluster of strong LLM-coordination baselines within the same $0.1$-score envelope; the strict dominance visible in HP-SPGG and Concordia does not persist out of scope. This is exactly the boundary that the prior-factorisation lower bound (Theorem~\ref{thm:lower-no-pf}) predicts: the exponential separation requires PF. The framework therefore degrades gracefully rather than catastrophically and remains in win-count parity with the strongest baselines, with full per-(backbone, scenario) breakdown deferred to Appendix~\ref{app:sotopia-detail}.

\section{Discussion, Limitations, and Conclusion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Summary.} We have introduced H-PSMG and H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+}, an algorithm family for centralised multi-agent LLM coordination under unknown personas and dynamics. The factored posterior is exact under three structural conditions and yields exponential storage savings in $n$ together with a Bayesian regret bound that is exponentially tighter than the prior-factorisation lower bound. The type-discrimination bonus reduces burn-in regret by an $\Omega(|\Theta_i|^n)$ factor on a deep-trap family. Across three LLM benchmarks of increasing wildness -- HP-SPGG, Concordia, SOTOPIA-Hard -- the algorithm family beats the strongest published LLM-coordination baselines by $6$--$12\times$ where the theoretical preconditions hold (HP-SPGG), wins or ties oracle on $16/16$ Concordia configurations, and remains within $0.1$ of the best alternative on every backbone in the out-of-scope dyadic-negotiation setting (SOTOPIA-Hard) where it leads the largest scenario family while losing the strict dominance the theory guarantees only under prior factorisation -- graceful degradation rather than catastrophic failure as the preconditions weaken.

\paragraph{Limitations.} (i) The regret guarantees assume a tabular state-action space and a discrete persona archetype set; the function-approximation generalisation FA-H-PSMG\textsuperscript{+} (Appendix~\ref{app:fa-hpsmg}) extends to continuous spaces but inherits function-approximation error. (ii) The CCE oracle is assumed deterministic and measurable (CSR); randomised CCE-tie-breaking would alter the burn-in constants. (iii) Personas are sampled once per episode and held fixed; non-stationary personas are out of scope. (iv) The persona-likelihood scoring requires next-token-probability access from the LLM backbone, which all four backbones we tested support; black-box-only LLMs would require a wrapper. (v) The bonus weight $\beta$ is held fixed at $0.25$ across all substrates and backbones; per-substrate adaptation could further improve burn-in performance but at the cost of substrate-specific tuning.

\paragraph{Future work.} Decentralised HT-MG-BC (each agent maintains its own posterior over the others), beyond-TI dynamics (persona-coupled transitions), and adaptive-$\beta$ schedules informed by per-episode posterior concentration estimates are natural next steps. The function-approximation generalisation (Appendix~\ref{app:fa-hpsmg}) is fully worked out and ready for empirical study at scale.
